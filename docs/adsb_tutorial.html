Five practical techniques from flight debriefs: altitude filters, airport traffic isolation, extended track, direct-clearance cone, and crossing restrictions.

Center on your target airport and set filter from 0 ft up to around 5,000 ft AGL (higher for mountain airports). Only arrivals, departures, and pattern traffic survive the filter.

40000
Lower filter stays at 0 ft always. Drag the upper slider down or click the preset. High-altitude en-route traffic disappears, leaving only the airport vicinity.
Mountain tip In high-elevation areas like the US mountain west, raise the upper filter — e.g. Denver KDEN sits at 5,431 ft MSL, so traffic at "5,000 ft AGL" is actually 10,000+ ft MSL. Adjust accordingly.
